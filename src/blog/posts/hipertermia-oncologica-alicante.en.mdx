If you're looking for an oncological hyperthermia centre in Alicante, Clínica Les Lilas is currently the only clinic specialised in this therapy in the entire province and on the Costa Blanca. Located in San Juan de Alicante, it offers [electromodulated oncological hyperthermia](/blog/hipertermia-oncologica#what-is-electromodulated-oncological-hyperthermia) as a complementary treatment to chemotherapy and radiotherapy, within an individualised medical protocol coordinated with the patient's oncologist.

There's no need to travel to Madrid or Barcelona to access this technology. Patients from Alicante, Elche, Torrevieja, Benidorm, Murcia and the entire region now have a reference centre in their area. This page answers the most frequently asked questions about availability, access and the treatment protocol in Alicante.

## Where is oncological hyperthermia available in Alicante?

Clínica Les Lilas is located at **Avinguda de la Diagonal, 4, Sant Joan d'Alacant** (San Juan de Alicante), a few minutes from the centre of Alicante with direct access from the N-332 and AP-7 motorway.

It is the only centre that offers electromodulated oncological hyperthermia on a specialised basis in the province of Alicante. This means there is currently no other private centre in the area with a structured hyperthermia programme as a complementary oncology treatment.

The clinic has a trilingual team — Spanish, French and English — serving both local patients and foreign residents of the Costa Blanca.

**How to get there**

- From the centre of Alicante: 10 minutes by car
- From Elche: 20 minutes
- From Benidorm: 40 minutes
- From Murcia: 55 minutes
- From Torrevieja: 35 minutes

Parking available in the area.

## Why aren't there more centres in the area?

Electromodulated oncological hyperthermia is a specialised technology that requires specific equipment, advanced medical training and rigorous clinical protocols. In Spain, the offer is concentrated mainly in Madrid and Barcelona, with occasional presence in other large cities.

This scarcity in the Alicante area is precisely why Clínica Les Lilas represents a differential opportunity for patients from the entire Valencian Community and Murcia: access to a complementary oncology therapy with [solid scientific evidence](/blog/hipertermia-oncologica#scientific-evidence-what-the-studies-say), without long journeys or coordination with centres in other cities.

For patients who already have their conventional treatments at Alicante hospitals — such as the University Hospital of San Juan, the General Hospital of Alicante or private clinics in the area — the proximity of Clínica Les Lilas facilitates the integration of hyperthermia into their treatment schedule.

## Who can access the treatment?

Access to treatment at Clínica Les Lilas is open to:

**Oncology patients on active treatment**
People receiving chemotherapy, radiotherapy or immunotherapy who wish to incorporate hyperthermia as complementary support to enhance treatment effectiveness and improve tolerance.

**Patients between treatment cycles**
Hyperthermia can also be integrated during rest periods between cycles, according to medical indication.

**Patients in post-treatment follow-up**
In some cases, hyperthermia can be useful as support in the follow-up phase, following medical assessment.

**Foreign residents of the Costa Blanca**
The clinic serves English- and French-speaking patients with the same treatment offer and in their language.

It is not necessary for the patient's oncologist to have expressly referred them to the clinic. Patients can contact directly. At the first consultation, the indication is evaluated and contact is established with the referring oncology team to coordinate the protocol.

## What the process is like from the first consultation

**Step 1 — Initial contact**
The patient contacts the clinic by WhatsApp, phone or web form. They receive a response from the team within 24 hours.

**Step 2 — First medical consultation**
A complete evaluation is carried out: tumour type, stage, treatments in progress, medication and general condition. Available oncology documentation is reviewed. This consultation determines whether hyperthermia is indicated and in what format.

**Step 3 — Coordination with the oncologist**
The clinic contacts the patient's referring oncologist to inform them of the proposed protocol and ensure compatibility with conventional treatment.

**Step 4 — Start of the protocol**
Sessions are scheduled to fit the patient's calendar and chemotherapy or radiotherapy cycles. The [standard protocol of 10 to 12 sessions](/tratamientos/hipertermia-oncologica) is applied with a frequency of 1 to 2 sessions per week.

**Step 5 — Follow-up**
Throughout the protocol, periodic reviews are carried out to evaluate the response and adjust the plan if necessary.

## Is it worth travelling from other provinces?

This is a legitimate question for patients living in Murcia, Valencia, Castellón or bordering areas.

The answer depends on each clinical case, but there are some factors that usually tip the balance:

**Manageable frequency.** The protocol is 1 to 2 sessions per week. It's not daily treatment. For many patients who already make periodic trips to their oncology centres, adding a weekly session to San Juan de Alicante is logistically viable.

**Lack of nearby alternative.** If the patient has already searched for hyperthermia centres in their province and found none, Clínica Les Lilas is in many cases the closest option in south-eastern Spain.

**Documented therapeutic value.** Oncological hyperthermia has sufficient scientific evidence for certain tumour types to consider the journey as an investment in treatment.

In any case, the first consultation can be carried out by video call to assess the indication before committing to any travel.
