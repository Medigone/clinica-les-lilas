## What is regenerative medicine?

Regenerative medicine is a rapidly expanding medical field that aims to restore, repair or replace damaged tissues and organs. Unlike conventional approaches that primarily treat symptoms, regenerative medicine seeks to address root causes by stimulating the body's natural repair mechanisms.

This therapeutic field rests on several pillars: cell therapies, growth factors, bio-stimulation and tissue support protocols.

## Main therapeutic approaches

### Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)

PRP is obtained from the patient's own blood. After centrifugation, the platelet-concentrated plasma is re-injected into the area to be treated. Platelets release growth factors that stimulate tissue regeneration.

PRP indications include:

- Tendon and ligament injuries
- Osteoarthritis and joint pain
- Post-surgical recovery
- Skin regeneration

### Bio-stimulation and growth factors

Beyond PRP, bio-stimulation protocols use combinations of growth factors, bioactive peptides and targeted nutrients to support cellular repair at a systemic level.

### Metabolic and cellular support

Regenerative medicine also integrates a metabolic dimension: optimization of cellular repair pathways through adapted nutritional support, targeted supplementation and complementary intravenous therapies.

## Concrete clinical applications

The applications of regenerative medicine are numerous and constantly evolving:

- **Orthopedics and rheumatology**: treatment of joint, tendon and muscle injuries without invasive surgery.
- **Anti-aging medicine**: stimulation of cellular renewal and improvement of tissue quality.
- **Post-treatment support**: supporting recovery after intensive treatments (chemotherapy, surgery).
- **Sports medicine**: accelerating injury healing and optimizing recovery.
